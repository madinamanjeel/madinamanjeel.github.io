Our Heritage

The Journey from Munshi Bari to Madina Manjeel

In the quiet village of South Adampur stood a modest homestead known as Munshi Bari. Here lived Monsur Munshi with his wife Shafia Khatun and their three sons — Abdur Rob Munshi, Salam Munshi, and Md Abdul Khaleque.

Abdur Rob Munshi was known for his calm personality and wisdom, a respected figure in the locality.

Salam Munshi played a key role in maintaining family bonds and the homestead.

Shafia Khatun was a kind-hearted woman respected in the community.

They were a family deeply rooted in agriculture. Life changed dramatically when Md Abdul Khaleque lost his father in childhood. Defying odds, he pursued education and got admitted into City College in Dhaka. During his time there, he joined the Bangladesh Air Force.

He later married Shariatunnesa Madina, daughter of Fazlul Haque Mia and Umme Salma of Dashmina Talukdar Bari.

The couple was blessed with two sons — Darul Ahmed and Mashayekh Ahmed, who followed in their father's footsteps and joined the Air Force in 2006 and 2010 respectively.

In 2009, they purchased 20 decimals of land in Dashmina from Mrs. Wajedunnesa. They initially lived in a tin-roofed house.

With many legal struggles, the family laid the foundation of their new home — Madina Manjeel — in 2010. The construction went on for years.

On 22nd September 2016, the family moved permanently into Madina Manjeel, celebrating the occasion with a prayer event.

What once had a pond and a ditch now bloomed into a beautiful garden in 2024 — with mango, jackfruit, coconut, guava, rose, banana, drumstick trees and vegetables thriving together.

Next Generation

In 2014, Darul Ahmed married Farjana Afrin. They are proud parents of Afia Ahmed Adiba and Nusaiba Jannat Alifa.

In 2022, Mashayekh Ahmed married Lima Akter of Bauphal. Their son, Ahmad Muttaqeen, continues the family’s growing story.

Home and Surroundings

Today, Madina Manjeel is under full surveillance with 6 CCTV cameras and is home to birds like cockatiels, budgerigars, pigeons, ducks, and chickens.
