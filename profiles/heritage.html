Our Heritage

The Early Days in South Adampur

Munshi Bari (Original)

Our story begins in the village of South Adampur, where my grandfather, Monsur Munshi, lived with his wife, Shafia Khatun.

Shafia Khatun

They were the proud parents of three sons: Abdur Rob Munshi, Salam Munshi, and Md Abdul Khaleque. All of them were involved in agriculture.

The Munshi Brothers

Abdur Rob Munshi & Salam Munshi

The eldest sons of Monsur Munshi and Shafia Khatun were Abdur Rob Munshi and Salam Munshi. They both dedicated their lives to agriculture, contributing to the family's livelihood through their hard work in the fields.

Tragedy struck early in the life of their younger brother, Md Abdul Khaleque, when he lost his father at a young age. Despite this hardship, he was determined to pursue education.

Abdul Khaleque Munshi

Md Abdul Khaleque's Journey

Through his own perseverance and hard work, Md Abdul Khaleque moved to Dhaka and enrolled in City College. While studying there, a significant turn occurred in his life as he joined the Bangladesh Air Force. He later married Shariatunnesa Madina, a daughter from the esteemed Talukdar Bari of Dashmina. Her parents were Fazlul Haque Mia and Umme Salma.

Md Abdul Khaleque, Shariatunnesa Madina and Darul Ahmed

Together, Abdul Khaleque and Shariatunnesa were blessed with two sons, Darul Ahmed and Mashayekh Ahmed. Continuing the tradition of service, both Darul Ahmed and Mashayekh Ahmed followed in their father's footsteps and joined the Bangladesh Air Force in 2006 and 2010, respectively.

The Foundation of Madina Manjeel

A new landmark in our family's history was established on May 26, 2009, when Md Abdul Khaleque and Shariatunnesa Madina purchased twenty decimal of land in the Dashmina Upazila Sadar. Following Mashayekh Ahmed's joining the Air Force in 2010, they laid the foundation of what would become known as Madina Manjeel on this very land.

Initially, the family resided in an old tin house on the property.

Madina Manjeel (Old Tin House)

Building Madina Manjeel

Madina Manjeel (Construction Phase)

The journey to build a permanent structure was fraught with legal challenges. However, perseverance prevailed, and in 2016, they finally moved into their newly constructed building, establishing their permanent residence at Madina Manjeel.

To commemorate this significant milestone, a Dua (prayer) ceremony was organized on September 22, 2016.

Dua Ceremony at Madina Manjeel (September 22, 2016)

The property initially consisted of a tin house, a ditch, and a pond. Over time, significant changes were made. The ditch was filled to make way for the new building.

Transformation and Growth

More recently, in 2024, the pond was filled and transformed into a flourishing fruit orchard. This garden now boasts a variety of high-quality fruit trees and vegetables.

The family continued to grow. Darul Ahmed married Farjana Afrin in 2014, and they are now blessed with two daughters, Afia Ahmed Adiba and Nusaiba Jannat Alifa. Mashayekh Ahmed married Lima Akter, who hails from Bauphal, in 2022, and they have a son named Ahmad Muttaqeen.

Present Day at Madina Manjeel

Today, Madina Manjeel is equipped with modern security, including six CCTV cameras. The household also cherishes the company of various pets, including cockatiels, budgerigars, pigeons, ducks, and chickens, adding life and vibrancy to our family home.
